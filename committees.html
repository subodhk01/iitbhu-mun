UNITED NATION SECURITY COUNCIL
This committee is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations whose primary objective is to ensure international peace and security, recommend admissions of new UN members to the General Assembly, and to approve any changes to the UN Charter. It has a total of 15 members, out of which 5 are permanent members, and the rest 10 are non-permanent members which are appointed from the General Assembly for a tenure of 2 years.
